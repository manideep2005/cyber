The Many Faces of Cyberbullying

Understanding tactics used is essential. Cyberbullying takes many forms — recognize them to protect yourself and others.

01

Harassment & Threats

Repeated sending of offensive or threatening messages

02

Cyberstalking

Monitoring and following someone's online activity obsessively

03

Doxxing

Publishing private personal information without consent

04

Impersonation

Creating fake profiles to damage someone's reputation

05

Exclusion & Shaming

Deliberately ostracizing or publicly humiliating someone

You're Not Alone

Support
Is Available

Taking the first step toward healing requires courage — but you don't have to face this alone. Immediate help, professional guidance, and community support are within reach.

1

Document Everything

Screenshot and save all evidence of bullying before blocking the offender.

2

Tell a Trusted Adult

Speak to a parent, teacher, counselor, or any trusted person in your life.

3

Report on the Platform

Use built-in reporting tools on the platform where the bullying occurred.

4

Seek Professional Help

A counselor or therapist can provide structured support for emotional recovery.

How To Stay Safe

How Can You
Stay Safe?

Simple, consistent habits dramatically reduce your exposure to online threats and harassment.

01

Update Your Software Regularly

Enable automatic updates for your OS, apps, and browser. Security patches close vulnerabilities attackers exploit.

02

Think Before You Click

Pause before clicking links or opening attachments, even from known senders. Verify suspicious requests through a different channel.

03

Guard Your Personal Information

Limit what you share publicly online. Oversharing enables stalkers, impersonators, and targeted harassment campaigns.

04

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Use a password manager to generate complex passwords. Enable two-factor authentication on all important accounts.

05

Audit Your Privacy Settings

Review privacy settings on every platform. Restrict who can message you, view your content, or tag you in posts.

06

Block & Report, Don't Engage

Never argue with a bully online. Engaging amplifies conflict. Block immediately, report, and preserve evidence.
